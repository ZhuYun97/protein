Multi-objective Optimization Strategy for Continuous Drilling Parameters of Superalloys
Qi Wang\(^{1,2}\) · Xi Chen\(^{2}\) · Qinglong An\(^{1}\) · Ming Chen\(^{1}\) · Hun Guo\(^{2}\) · Yafeng He\(^{2}\)
Received: 19 August 2023 / Revised: 13 December 2023 / Accepted: 17 December 2023 / Published online: 24 January 2024 © The Author(s), under exclusive licence to Korean Society for Precision Engineering 2024

Abstract
There are a large number of holes to be machined on aeroengine components such as blisks, casings, etc. In order to ensure position accuracy, these holes usually need to be drilled continuously in one process. To ensure the machining quality of holes, either replacing the cutting tools in advance leads to an increase in manufacturing costs, or adjusting process parameters leads to a decrease in production efficiency, which is difficult to meet the requirements of efficient and low-cost manufacturing. In response to this issue, this paper proposes a multi-objective optimization strategy for the process parameters of porous continuous drilling of superalloys alloys. A unified mathematical model for multi-objective optimization of drilling parameters has been established, and a tool life prediction model based on machining parameters and a machining process energy consumption model have been established as objective functions. The proposed optimization strategy can select different optimization strategies for different optimization objectives, including: maximum tool life, minimum machining energy consumption, and multi-objective drilling parameter optimization. Finally, experimental verification was conducted on the proposed strategy, and the results showed that the proposed optimization strategy can significantly reduce drilling processing energy consumption and increase the service life of drilling tools.
Keywords Multi-objective parameter optimization · Tool life modelling · Energy consumption modelling · NSGA – II · Drilling

1 Introduction
Parts such as nickel base superalloy disks, blisk, and aeroengine housings usually have a large number of hole structures, which usually need to be drilled [1]. However, due to the high hardness and high-temperature softening characteristics of superalloys, machining efficiency is low and tool wear is serious [2]. Therefore, optimizing the drilling process parameters is the key to improving the efficiency and quality of superalloys processing [3].
In actual machining, given the workpiece, tool, and machining conditions, the drilling process parameters
directly determine the efficiency and quality of the machining [4]. The setting of drilling process parameters is often determined by process personnel based on experience. Once defects occur during on-site processing, the overall cutting parameters are reduced [5]. This not only leads to low machining efficiency, but also makes it difficult to ensure machining quality [6]. Therefore, scholars have conducted extensive research on optimizing drilling process parameters to address this issue. The existing methods can be divided into two categories [7]: one is to select the optimal parameters, that is, to select the optimal level of each parameter from a given combination of several process parameters; Another type is process parameter optimization, which involves obtaining the optimal values of each parameter within a certain range of process parameters.
The selection of process parameters is usually based on design of experiments methods [8] such as orthogonal experiments or corresponding surfaces to obtain the main effect and optimal level of target variables related to hole processing quality [9] processing efficiency [10] or production cost [11], and then select the optimal level of each
process parameter [12]. Abdelhafeez et al. [13] carried out drilling experiments on three types of aluminum alloys, and studied the effects of parameter on burr size, hole diameter and tool wear through response surface methodology. Kalita and Bhargab [14] reviewed the research on optimizing drilling process parameters using orthogonal experimental methods. Hassanalideh and Gholampour [15] used the finite element method to simulate the drilling process on bone materials. By studying the effects of changes in three parameters: maximum temperature, thrust, torque, and temperature durability, the optimization of bone drilling parameters was achieved.
In addition, another commonly used method for selecting drilling parameters is grey correlation analysis [16], which is used to obtain the correlation between different levels of parameters and target variables, and then select the optimal parameter level [17]. Arunkumar et al. [18] studied the influence of cutting speed, feed speed and cutting fluid pressure on the hole wall roughness and the cylindricity and roundness of the hole during the drilling process of AISI 1045 steel by using the orthogonal test method, and obtained the optimal parameter settings by using the gray correlation analysis. Pramanik et al. [19] studied the effects of feed, rotational speed, and drill tip angle on burr height, thrust, torque, and chip during the drilling process, and obtained the optimal parameters using grey correlation analysis.
The process parameter optimization method usually consists of two steps [20]: the first step is to establish a mathematical model between the optimization goal and the control variables [21]. The commonly used modeling methods include multiple regression, response surface methodology, neural network, fuzzy logic, etc.; The second step is to find the optimal solution of the model [22], that is, to find the extreme point of the target variable. Davim and António [23] conducted drilling experiments and numerical simulations to investigate the connection between cutting force, tool wear, and surface roughness. They employed evolutionary strategies to derive optimal solutions for predicting cutting speed, feed rate, and tool life, which hold industrial significance. Balaji et al. [24] studied the influence of spindle speed, feed speed and drill spiral angle on hole wall roughness, flank wear and vibration acceleration during the drilling process of steel AISI 304 through the Response surface methodology of central synthesis design, obtained the response surface models of different indicators and control factors, and used the multi response optimization method to obtain the optimized process parameters. Vinayagamoorthy et al. [25] used fuzzy logic to establish the relationship between hole defects, axial force, and torque during the drilling process of fiber reinforced materials, as well as cutting speed, feed rate, drill diameter, and tip angle. The optimized process parameters were obtained using fuzzy reasoning. Feng
et al. [26] established a competitive failure model for estimating the lifespan of drill bits, derived the reliability function and service life expression of drill bits, and used them for optimizing drilling parameters. Chang et al. [27] established a parameter model based on Taguchi's neural network method to optimize the drilling parameters of \(1\;\mathrm{mm}\) diameter holes in order to reduce the drilling cost of engineering plastics Polyether ether ketone (PEEK).
However, in the drilling process of superalloys, due to the complexity of material characteristics and the mutual influence between process parameters [28], single objective optimization methods often cannot meet the requirements. Multi objective optimization method that can consider multiple objective functions, by finding a set of optimal solutions to balance the contradictions between different objectives [29]. There are multiple optimizable goals in the superalloys drilling process, such as minimizing surface roughness [30], maximizing material removal rate [5] and minimizing tool wear [31]. Therefore, adopting multi-objective optimization methods can better solve problems in the drilling process of superalloys. Wang et al. [32] considered the impact of tool wear on drilling, established an energy consumption model for superalloys drilling process, and optimized drilling parameters with the goal of tool wear and processing energy consumption. Han et al. [33] proposed a variable parameter drilling method to improve the drilling efficiency and surface roughness of nickel base superalloy, establish the objective function of drilling time and surface roughness, and use the adaptive penalty method to solve the optimal drilling parameters.
In summary, a large amount of research has been conducted on the optimization methods of drilling process parameters, mainly focusing on finding the optimal parameters under specific single step drilling conditions. However, in actual drilling processing, the working conditions are mostly continuous drilling, and different processing stages require different technical requirements, that is, the objectives of optimizing processing parameters are different. How to select different optimization objectives at different processing stages and choose different optimization methods for different objectives is a problem that needs to be solved. Therefore, this article proposes a multi-objective optimization strategy for the continuous drilling process of porous superalloys. The proposed method can select different optimization objectives and strategies for different processing stages, rendering it highly applicable in actual processing conditions. The structure of the article is arranged as follows. Section 2 describes the optimization problem. In Sect. 3, a mathematical model for optimizing drilling parameters is established. In Sect. 4, the optimization program is described in detail. Then describe the experimental setup in Sect. 5, followed by a discussion of the optimization results in Sect. 6. Finally, conclusions will be provided in Sect. 7.

2 Problem Description
2 Problem DescriptionNickel base superalloy wheel disks, blisk, and aeroengine housings usually have high requirements on the surface quality and machining energy efficiency of holes. In machining, in order to better locate all the holes, continuous drilling is usually used for hole making operations [34]. However, the continuous drilling process is often accompanied by severe drill bit wear, resulting in high tool usage costs. To ensure the machining quality of holes, the process often faces problems such as high tool usage costs and low processing energy efficiency.
In order to address this issue, it is crucial to analyze the impact of various working conditions during the machining process on drilling processing. The relationship between cutting parameters, tool load, tool wear, processing time, processing energy efficiency, and tool cost in the continuous drilling is shown in Fig. 1. In theory, the maximum allowable feed rate should be applied to reduce processing time, but it is limited by tool load and tool wear [35]. Moreover, tool load, tool wear, processing time, processing energy efficiency, and tool life are dependent variables that are correlated with each other, which makes it difficult for commonly used single optimization methods to meet the parameter optimization requirements under multiple working conditions.
In actual machining, the spindle speed and feed rate are the main control factors for improving machining energy efficiency and decreasing tool costs. However, there is a contradictory relationship between higher processing energy efficiency and lower tool costs. Therefore, it is necessary to optimize the processing parameters through scientific methods to achieve better efficiency improvement and lower manufacturing costs. In response to this issue, this article proposes a multi-objective drilling process optimization strategy, which considers the drilling process of holes as a whole for global optimization. Different optimization strategies are selected for different optimization objectives to achieve the goal of reducing production costs and improving processing energy efficiency.

7 Conclusions
A multi-objective optimization strategy for the process parameters of porous continuous drilling of superalloys has been proposed. This optimization strategy aims to find the optimal cutting parameters for the continuous drilling process, in order to reduce manufacturing costs and improve machining efficiency. The main contributions and innovative points are summarized as follows:
(1) Compared with the optimization of drilling process parameters under specific conditions, the proposed multi-objective optimization strategy is more suitable for the actual continuous drilling situation of difficult to cut materials such as nickel based superalloys. (2) The established tool life and processing energy efficiency prediction model fully considers actual working conditions and is more suitable for predicting tool life and processing energy efficiency under actual working conditions. (3) The proposed optimization strategy can be optimized for different optimization objectives and has wider applicability. (4) The experimental verification results showed that the proposed optimization strategy can significantly reduce drilling processing energy consumption and increase the service life of drilling tools.
This study is an attempt to optimize the continuous drilling process parameters for superalloy materials, mainly addressing the main issues of high manufacturing costs and low processing efficiency in nickel based superalloys drilling. Tool life and processing energy efficiency are the main concerns of this work. Further research will be conducted on
more targets in the future, such as surface roughness, surface integrity, and shape and position accuracy of holes.
