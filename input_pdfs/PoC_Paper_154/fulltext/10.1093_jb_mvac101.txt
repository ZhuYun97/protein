","page_idx":10},{"type":"text","text":"When proteins are solubilized with a denaturing agent, it is thought that trypsin and the protein substrate can move freely, increasing the efficiency of the reaction. It is also thought that protein solubilization with a strong denaturing agent exposes the cleavage sites of the substrate proteins, making it easier for trypsin to attack them. In iSDAC, however, the proteins and cell pellets are not solubilized prior to trypsin digestion, but are dispersed in a denaturing agentfree buffer before the enzymatic reaction by sonication. In the iSDAC method, the surface layer of the denatured protein is cleaved early in the digestion, and then solubilization is accelerated as enzymatic digestion proceeds, and finally complete enzymatic digestion is probably achieved. The absence of denaturant may also contribute to the high digestion efficiency, since trypsin activity can be maintained for a long period of time.
