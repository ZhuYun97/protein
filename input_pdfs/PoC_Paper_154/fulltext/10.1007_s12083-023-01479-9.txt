Clustering helps create scalable and robust network infrastructure. It enhances the network lifetime while reducing power consumption. However, clustered WSNs suffer from imbalanced load distribution, inadequate cluster head (CH) allocation and uneven energy consumption issues. In case of improper clustering, nodes get prematurely dead due to excessive energy consumption. This adversely influences the network duration and worsen its performance. To overcome these concerns, this paper attempts to improve the clustering quality through optimal cluster head selection and an advanced cluster formation technique. As this paper tackles the clustering issue in WSNs, it introduces a clustering based routing protocol, named ROA-MOCT. In ROA-MOCT, the CH selection process is performed using the Raccoon optimization algorithm, whereas cluster formation is managed by each CH according to a multi-objective optimization technique. The ROA provides more accurate and precise solutions than other optimization algorithms. Together with the improved fitness function, these characteristics help selecting adequate cluster heads with high energy levels and improving clusters segregation. In ROA-MOCT, the cluster formation process is entrusted to the CHs. Each CH selects its cluster members based on three criteria: intra-cluster distance, distance from the energy gravity center and distance to the global center. This method enhances the clustering quality and improves the data flow in the network. The ROA-MOCT protocol was tested against LEACH and YSGAP protocols to prove its efficiency. The experiments were conducted using $\\mathrm{OMNET++}$ simulator and the INET platform and the assessment results showed major improvement over the two tested protocols regarding the network lifetime and its three metrics (FND, HND and LND), the number of received packet by the BS and the network residual energy. As future work, we intend to test the multi-objective optimization technique based cluster formation on other protocols such LEACH and others. Moreover, we intend to involve more criteria and parameters to study its effects on the clustering process and thus on the network lifetime and performance.
